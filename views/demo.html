2-Phase Calibration — Auto on Startup
👁
Phase 1 — Rest: Serial Monitor prints a dot every second while sampling. After 10 seconds it prints the captured baselines — Thumb, Index, and Middle frequency values side by side. Hand must stay completely still and flat the entire time.

Phase 2 — Pinch: Immediately after Phase 1 the prompt switches to pinch mode. Squeeze as hard as possible for 10 seconds. The firmware records the peak delta per channel and sets the detection threshold to 25% of that peak. After Phase 2 completes the Serial Monitor prints CALIBRATION DONE and live gesture classification begins.

Stage-by-Stage Walkthrough
1
~15 MIN · SENSOR HEALTH CHECK

Verify All Channels Are Live

Flash existing LDC firmware. Call RawData() in loop(). Open Serial Monitor at 115200 baud. Verify no channel shows 0, 4294967295, or 268435455 constantly. Bend each finger individually — only that channel should respond. This confirms sensor independence and correct wiring before anything else.

// Expected Serial Monitor output: Channel 0: 14523891 Channel 1: 11234567 Channel 2: 11892034 Channel 3: 12100456
2
~10 MIN · MATLAB LIVE VISUALIZATION

Show Real-Time Inductance Curves

Switch loop() to call MATLAB(). Run NUMChannelDataCollector.m — set port and numChannels=3. Three live subplots appear. Bend each finger one at a time and show that only that channel's trace moves, proving sensor independence.

Close the Arduino Serial Monitor before running MATLAB. Only one program can own the COM port at a time.
3
~15 MIN · DUAL-PHASE CALIBRATION + GESTURE DETECTION

Auto-Calibration then Live Classification

Flash GloveDemoV3.ino. Phase 1 — hold hand open and flat for 10 seconds. Phase 2 — squeeze a full hard pinch for 10 seconds. Both phases complete automatically. Live classification then runs at 10Hz with intensity scaled to your personal grip range. No hardcoded thresholds.

// After both phases complete: Thumb:65000000 Index:61900000 Middle:62500000 GESTURE: PINCH (72%)
4
~15 MIN · WIRELESS END-TO-END + ROBOTIC ARM

Full Pipeline — Two Xiaos, H-Bridge, DC Motors

Flash ArmReceiver_Motor_V3.ino to the second Xiao. Copy its MAC address into GloveDemoV3.ino and reflash. Pinch and watch the DC motors drive the robotic fingers closed proportionally. Release and the motors reverse to open position.

GESTURE : PINCH INTENSITY : 72% MOTOR PWM : 183 / 255 DIRECTION : FORWARD (closing fingers)
Demo Talk Track

5 Talking Points for Judges

#ShowSay
1 Point to glove + LDC board Stainless steel inductive threads are sewn into each finger. As a finger bends, the inductance changes. The LDC1614 converts those changes into a 28-bit number 10 times per second.
2 Phase 1 calibration video Before any gesture is detected the system calibrates itself. Phase 1 captures the resting baseline for each finger over 10 seconds. These numbers are unique to how this glove sits on this hand right now.
3 Phase 2 calibration video Phase 2 captures the full pinch range — squeeze as hard as possible for 10 seconds. The firmware records the peak delta per channel and derives detection thresholds from it automatically. No hardcoded values needed.
4 Arm demo video — vary pinch intensity The gesture and intensity are transmitted wirelessly in under 5 milliseconds over ESP-NOW. The arm Xiao maps intensity to a PWM duty cycle — a light pinch spins the motors slowly, a full pinch drives them at maximum speed. Releasing reverses the motors and the fingers open.
5 Show MATLAB CSV files Every session generates a CSV of raw sensor readings. These feed the neural network training pipeline — the model learns gesture classification from signal shape, not just magnitude, making it robust to different users and glove positions.
