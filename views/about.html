About the Project

Wearable Gesture Control
for Robotic Systems

A senior design capstone at NYIT proposing a smart textile glove capable of wirelessly controlling a robotic arm through dynamic hand-gesture recognition.

This paper presents a proposal for the development of a smart textile glove capable of wirelessly controlling a robotic arm through dynamic hand-gesture recognition. The glove integrates five inductive sensors threaded into the fabric — chosen for their flexibility and durability, allowing placement along fingers without risk of damage from repeated motion. Two Xiao C6 microcontrollers enable wireless communication between the glove and the robotic arm, allowing the user to operate the system through natural hand movements from a safe distance.

Why This Project Matters

Pharmaceutical research plays an important role in advancing global health, but the process of developing new medicines often exposes scientists to hazardous chemicals and biological agents. Many current robotic arm interfaces rely on controllers or software that are difficult to learn and are not ideal for tasks requiring natural, precise motion.

Our proposed control system aims to be intuitive, quick to learn, and adaptive — achieved through a gesture-detecting glove that allows the robotic arm to move at the same speed and direction as the user's hand movements.
