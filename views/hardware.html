Inductive Sensor Design

Conductive Thread Coils

Each inductive sensor is made from stainless steel conductive thread sewn into the glove fabric. As a finger bends, the distance between the coil and a metal reference changes — causing a measurable shift in the coil's magnetic inductance.

Sensors are positioned along the finger from the MCP (metacarpophalangeal) to DIP (distal interphalangeal) joint. Each coil has two wire ends connected to INxA and INxB on the LDC1614EVM — polarity does not matter.

Coil Geometry

Loops per coil3–5 minimum
Loop spacing (s)~2mm (smaller = stronger signal)
Thread materialStainless steel conductive thread
